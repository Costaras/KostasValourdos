Project Summary

This project conducts a comprehensive analysis of COVID-19 data to extract meaningful insights regarding infection rates, death rates, and vaccination progress across various locations and timeframes. The SQL queries and views are designed to evaluate several key metrics. The project begins with analyzing the likelihood of death after contracting COVID-19 in specific locations, exemplified by Greece, by calculating the deaths per 100 cases. It further examines the percentage of the population infected by comparing total cases to population sizes. Additionally, the project ranks countries by their highest infection ratios and death counts in relation to their populations, providing a global perspective on the pandemic's impact. The analysis also includes the creation of rolling sums and death rates on a global scale, allowing for a dynamic understanding of the situation as it evolves.

Moreover, the project emphasizes the relationship between population size and vaccination efforts. By utilizing common table expressions (CTEs) and temporary tables, it calculates rolling sums of vaccinations to track progress over time. This is complemented by creating views for visualizations, which streamline the representation of the data for better interpretation and decision-making. Specifically, views like PercentPopulationVaccinated, GlobalCovidCasualties, ContinentCovidCasualties, and DeathPerPopulation facilitate ongoing analysis and reporting. These views aggregate data at different levels, such as global, continental, and country-specific, providing a multi-faceted approach to understanding the pandemic's trends and informing public health strategies.
